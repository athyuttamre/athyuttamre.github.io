Balance

Interaction Design · Fall 2015

Balance is a money-tracking app for users with variable income. I designed and prototyped the idea for an Interaction Design studio last fall, and am hoping to build it one day for iOS using the Plaid API.

Problem

How can we help students and folks with variable income keep track of their money?

Students, hourly workers, Uber drivers; regular money tracking apps don't work for them. Mint is too complicated, Level and so on make assumptions.

I had spent hours on Reddit researching r/personalfinance. I'd tried budgeting, and so on, but really all I needed to know was how much money I had, and where it was going.

Research

Talking to college students. Characteristically, they were folks with variable income, volatile spending, occasional planning. Some had regular jobs, some got boosts of money from parents a few times a year, and so on. - They wanted to know how much money they have, where it was going. - Trends to compare to past months, and knowing important things ahead of time. Had too much on plate already, no time to plan. - Occasional tips to help save more.

Solution

Features: - Bank accounts; A simple way to keep track of your money. Works with over 6000+ bank accounts, powered by Plaid. -- Calendar view to see your payments in context. -- Integrations with Venmo, Square Cash and so on; often pay $100 in dinner, but in reality your friends are paying you back. (Show BoA or Mint screenshot?) - Trends and categories - Challenges to keep you accounted - Notifications - Email Digest
